Quality Control Checklist

You're not delivering data. You're delivering confidence. Don't skip this.

🖨️ Print-optimised. Complete before sending any deliverable.
Survey ID: _________________________    Reviewer: _________________________    Date: _________________________
Section A — Data Coverage Verification
  • GPS track is continuous across the survey route Open route on map. Look for gaps, jumps, or sections where the track doubles back unexpectedly. Any gap >50 m should be noted or re-surveyed.
  • All expected structures are present as POIs Cross-reference against the known route structure list (if available). Compare POI count with expected count from desktop review of satellite imagery.
  • No POIs at coordinates 0,0 Sort the CSV by latitude — any rows at exactly 0.000000 indicate GPS was not connected at capture time.
  • Chainage is continuous and increasing In the road profile CSV, check that chainage values increase monotonically. A reset to zero mid-route indicates a GPS outage caused a segment split.
Section B — GNSS Accuracy Validation
  • RTK fix confirmed for critical sections Check the survey log for periods where GPS degraded from RTK to SBAS. Flag these in the deliverable comments. Positional accuracy for those sections is ±1–3 m, not ±2 cm.
  • No large coordinate jumps (>5 m in <1 second) These indicate a GPS glitch. Check the raw GeoJSON — a single point wildly off the route. Remove the outlier POI or note it as GPS artifact.
  • GPS timestamp is plausible Verify survey date/time in metadata.json matches the actual survey date. A wrong system clock will corrupt all timestamps.
Section C — Clearance Measurement Validation
  • Calibration control check Locate the first POI captured during the on-site calibration pass. Its height should match the known reference structure within ±50 mm. If outside tolerance, the entire survey ground reference may be incorrect.
  • No clearance values <0.5 m unless flagged as intentional Sub-0.5 m values typically indicate a ground reflection or noise return. Review the corresponding photo. If no photo or obviously wrong, mark the POI for review.
  • No clearance values >18 m unless route has very tall structures Values above 18 m may indicate sky returns that slipped through the ignoreAbove filter. Verify against photos.
  • Each clearance POI has a photo Photos are the evidence layer. Missing photos weaken the deliverable. For any POI without a photo, add a note explaining why (camera failure, obstruction).
  • Clearance anomalies reviewed and flagged Any measurement that is >1 m different from adjacent measurements of the same structure type should be reviewed and either confirmed or marked as suspect.
Section D — Deliverable Packaging
  • metadata.json is present and complete Check: operator name, survey date, ground reference value, app version, calibration method are all populated.
  • All requested formats are present in the ZIP Open the ZIP and confirm every requested format file exists and is non-zero bytes.
  • Re-import test passed Drag the delivery ZIP into MeasurePRO. Confirm the survey loads correctly. This validates the archive is intact and the client can independently verify.
  • Deliverable sent with this QC sign-off attached Include a completed copy of this checklist (PDF or printed scan) with the deliverable. It documents due diligence.
