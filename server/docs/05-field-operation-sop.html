Field Operation SOP

Standard Operating Procedure for every survey drive. This is the real product.

PHASE A

Start Sequence

  1. Power on Duro GNSS. Wait for RTK indicator to turn green (60–90 seconds). If only SBAS available, note in survey comments — do not proceed for precision engineering surveys.
  2. Power on all lasers. Confirm each power LED is active before connecting via app.
  3. Launch MeasurePRO in Chrome or Edge. Navigate to the Survey screen.
  4. Connect vertical laser — Settings → Laser → Select Port. Aim at a stationary surface and confirm stable readings appear in the Measurement card. A reading that jumps or shows -- every other second indicates a connection or threshold problem.
  5. Connect GPS. Confirm the GPS status indicator is green and the map is updating. If Duro is connected via TCP, the app will display "RTK Fixed" in the GNSS panel.
  6. Create a new survey — tap "New Survey", enter the road/route name, operator name, and any client reference. This creates the survey record that all POIs will be linked to.
  7. Run sensor validation pass — drive 100–200 m on a known section of road. Confirm laser readings are consistent, GPS track is drawing correctly, and there are no red error indicators.
PHASE B

Data Capture

Speed and Range

ParameterRecommendedNotes
Survey speed10–40 km/hSlower for congested areas or complex geometry; reduce below 20 km/h near critical structures
Laser sampling rateContinuousApp captures every valid return; speed affects spatial density (faster = fewer samples per metre)
Distance from overhead obstacleDirect overheadVehicle centreline should pass under the lowest point of the obstacle for accurate clearance
Overlap on repeat passesMin 50% lateral overlap for LiDARSingle-pass sufficient for vertical clearance laser only

Capturing a POI

  • The app auto-captures a POI when the laser detects a reading within your configured clearance alert thresholds
  • Manual capture: press Space or tap the Capture button to force a POI at the current position
  • After capture, the POI modal opens — confirm the type (bridge, overpass, wire, signalization, etc.), add notes, and take additional photos if needed
  • Lateral captures: [ = left lateral, ] = right lateral, \ = total width, ' = rear overhang
  • Photos are captured from the camera assigned to that position (front/left/right/rear) automatically

When to Redo a Pass

  • GPS signal was lost for >5 seconds during a critical section — the affected segment will show no coordinates
  • Laser showed continuous -- (no-target) through an important structure — check ignoreAbove threshold
  • Vehicle speed exceeded 60 km/h on a section with structures at <0.5 m spatial intervals
  • Mount shifted during transit — visible as sudden step-change in readings by >100 mm
  • LiDAR data gap: the companion service crashed mid-capture and recovery was not possible

OPTIONAL

Road Profile Recording — Grade, Chainage, K-Factor

Road Profile records the longitudinal profile of the road as you drive: chainage (cumulative distance in metres), grade (slope as a %), and K-factor (rate of grade change). Requires Swift Navigation Duro with RTK Fixed fix.

  1. Confirm Duro is connected and RTK Fixed is shown (green indicator)
  2. Open Settings → Road Profile → Enable Road Profile Recording
  3. Start a survey and press Alt+3 to begin logging
  4. The Road Profile panel updates in real time during the drive
  5. Export via Survey Manager: CSV / GeoJSON / Shapefile / DXF / LandXML / ZIP
Duro only: Road Profile is unavailable with USB GPS, Bluetooth GPS, or browser geolocation. If the module does not appear, check that the Duro is the active GPS source and has RTK Fixed.
PHASE C

Edge Cases — Where 80% of Failures Happen

Rain and Wet Conditions

Light rain: amplitude filter will increase rejection rate (more -- readings). Lower the amplitude threshold slightly. Heavy rain: laser beam is attenuated; do not survey — readings will be 0.2–0.5 m lower than actual clearance due to rain-drop reflections. Dry all optical surfaces before connecting.

Dust

Airborne dust scatters the laser beam similarly to rain. In high-dust conditions (gravel roads, construction zones), increase the ignoreBelow threshold to 0.3 m above normal to reject false-floor dust returns. Check lens cleanliness before and after dusty sections.

GNSS Signal Loss

When Duro stops sending data for >5 seconds, USB GPS takes over automatically. If USB GPS also fails, the app continues capturing laser data but POIs will have no coordinates. These can be manually located later on the map if you noted the chainage or time. For tunnels: pre-record the entry and exit coordinates — the app will not interpolate through the tunnel.

Narrow Corridors (alleyways, cuttings)

Lateral lasers may get confused by nearby walls that are closer than the actual obstacle. Use single-laser mode in narrow corridors and enter the wall distance as a manual offset. The LiDAR may produce dense overlapping returns — reduce scan density or switch to static capture mode.

Bridges and Multi-Level Obstacles

On structures with multiple clearance planes (bridge deck + beam + pipe), the laser returns the first (lowest) object. If you need all clearance levels, take multiple passes at different lateral offsets. Ensure ignoreAbove is set above the highest obstacle you need to detect — otherwise the bridge deck return will be rejected as sky.

Strong Direct Sunlight

On the LDM71: direct sunlight can overwhelm the receiver. The amplitude dB reading will drop sharply. Increase the minimum amplitude threshold, or plan surveys in the morning / overcast conditions for consistent results.

High-Voltage Power Lines

The laser does not distinguish between power lines and other overhead obstacles. Power line clearances are captured correctly but the operator must classify the POI type correctly (powerLine / wire) for the export to flag it accurately in the clearance report. If a line sags in hot weather, re-survey in the hottest part of the day for conservative clearance figures.

Swept Path / Articulated Vehicles in Turns

The current Swept Path module uses simplified turn physics (90° circular arc approximation). For complex articulated routes with tight turns, treat the analysis as a conservative estimate and validate with physical pilot run before permitting. See replit.md for full MVP limitation notes.
