Maintenance & Storage Protocol

Skip maintenance and your hardware reputation dies. Follow this and your kit lasts years.

NEVER: Use compressed air directly on laser lens. Never submerge any unit. Never store in a vehicle parked in direct sun (temperatures exceed laser and battery operating limits).

Cleaning Schedule

ItemFrequencyMethod
Laser lens (vertical / lateral / rear) After every survey Soft lens cloth (microfibre) with optical lens cleaner. Do NOT use paper towels or abrasive cloths. Wipe in one direction only — do not rub in circles.
USB / DB9 connectors Weekly Blow with canned air from 20 cm distance. Apply contact cleaner if corrosion visible. Reseat connectors firmly after cleaning.
GNSS antenna connector and cable Weekly Check RF connector for moisture or corrosion. Apply waterproof connector grease to exposed RF connectors on the antenna end. Tighten hand-tight only — do not overtighten SMA/TNC connectors.
LiDAR rotating head (Hesai Pandar40P) Monthly [FILL IN — refer to Hesai Pandar40P maintenance manual, Chapter X]. Remove dust from window with approved Hesai lens cloth only.
Mount bracket and bolts Weekly Check torque on all mount bolts. Vibration loosens hardware — do not assume tightness. Apply thread-lock (medium strength) on bolts exposed to constant vibration.
Controller device (laptop/tablet) Monthly Keyboard and port area: compressed air. Screen: screen-safe cloth. Check fan vents for dust accumulation — blocked vents cause thermal throttling during survey.

Storage Requirements

Temperature Range

  • Operating: [FILL IN — check each device datasheet, typically -10°C to +50°C]
  • Storage: [FILL IN — typically -20°C to +60°C]
  • Never store in a vehicle left in direct sunlight — interior temperature can exceed 80°C
  • In cold climates: allow units to warm to operating temperature before powering on (condensation risk)

Humidity and Moisture

  • Store in sealed case with silica gel desiccant packs (replace desiccant monthly)
  • After use in rain or high humidity: leave case open in dry environment for 2 hours before sealing
  • Lens caps on all lasers during storage — always
  • Connector covers on RF and USB ports during transport and storage

Transport Precautions

  • Use padded foam-lined case for all laser units — do not allow loose transport in the back of a vehicle
  • Duro GNSS receiver: foam-padded case; do not allow cable to remain under tension during transport
  • LiDAR (Hesai Pandar40P): [FILL IN — refer to Hesai transport guidelines]. The rotating head is sensitive to shock — always use original packing foam
  • Controller laptop: screen-down in padded bag; remove all USB dongles before transport to avoid port damage
  • Label all cases with equipment contents and operator contact information

Calibration Frequency

CalibrationFrequencyMethod
Ground reference (laser height above ground) Every deployment Aim laser at ground surface before departing yard. Capture 3 stable readings. Confirm value in Settings. Re-calibrate if mount has been removed or adjusted.
Calibration validation (known-clearance check) First pass on every survey Drive under a known-clearance structure (bridge with documented clearance). Confirm reading within ±50 mm of known value.
Amplitude filter baseline Weekly or after conditions change In standard dry conditions, run amplitude filter at default setting. After extended use in rain/dust, recalibrate baseline against clean-condition readings.
GNSS antenna alignment Monthly or after mount changes Verify Duro achieves RTK fix within 90 seconds in open sky. If consistently slow to acquire, check antenna cable and connector for damage.
LiDAR internal calibration Per Hesai schedule [FILL IN — refer to Hesai Pandar40P calibration manual]. Factory calibration certificate should accompany the unit.
Vehicle dimension profile After any vehicle change Update vehicle width, height, wheelbase, and overhang values in Settings. Incorrect vehicle profile will cause wrong lateral width and swept path calculations.

End-of-Day Shutdown

  1. Close current survey in app (tap Close Survey — wait for sync confirmation)
  2. Disconnect all lasers and GPS sources in app before physically unplugging
  3. Stop LiDAR Windows service if running
  4. Power off lasers
  5. Power off Duro GNSS
  6. Disconnect all USB cables — store in labelled velcro ties
  7. Fit lens caps to all laser apertures
  8. Place equipment in foam-padded cases with desiccant
  9. Charge controller device overnight
