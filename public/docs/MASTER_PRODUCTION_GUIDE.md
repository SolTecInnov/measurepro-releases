## 🎬 PRODUCTION DAY CHECKLIST

### Every Morning:

- [ ] **Weather check** - adjust schedule if needed
- [ ] **Equipment inventory** - verify all gear present
- [ ] **Crew call time** - everyone on time
- [ ] **Safety briefing** - 10 minutes before filming
- [ ] **Location walk-through** - establish shot list priorities
- [ ] **Camera tests** - white balance, exposure, focus
- [ ] **Audio check** - mic levels, no interference
- [ ] **Review shot cards** - understand day's objectives

### During Shooting:

- [ ] **Slate every shot** - scene, shot #, take #
- [ ] **Monitor footage** - check focus, exposure, composition
- [ ] **Log important takes** - note keeper or script supervisor
- [ ] **Backup footage** - download cards at lunch/end of day
- [ ] **Stay on schedule** - track time per setup
- [ ] **Communicate** - walkie-talkies, hand signals
- [ ] **Hydrate and feed crew** - breaks every 2-3 hours

### End of Day:

- [ ] **Review captured shots** - confirm all completed
- [ ] **Note any pickups needed** - add to next day's schedule
- [ ] **Equipment check-in** - verify nothing lost/damaged
- [ ] **Charge batteries overnight** - cameras, mics, lights, drone
- [ ] **Download and backup footage** - two copies minimum
- [ ] **Update production report** - shots captured, issues, notes
- [ ] **Prep for next day** - review call sheet, shot list

## 🎨 POST-PRODUCTION WORKFLOW

### Week 1: Assembly

**Day 1-2: Ingest & Organization**
- Import all footage into editing software (Premiere Pro / Final Cut Pro)
- Create folder structure: Scenes 1-11, B-Roll, Audio, Graphics
- Transcode to editing codec if needed (ProRes Proxy)
- Sync audio to video (PluralEyes or manual)
- Label and color-code clips by scene

**Day 3-5: Rough Cut**
- Assemble scenes in script order
- Select best takes for each shot
- Create 14-minute timeline
- Rough transitions (straight cuts)
- Placeholder for voiceover
- Client review screening

**Day 6-7: Client Feedback**
- Present rough cut to client
- Note requested changes
- Discuss pacing, tone, messaging
- Identify any missing shots

### Week 2: Refinement

**Day 8-9: Revised Cut**
- Incorporate client notes
- Refine pacing and timing
- Add B-roll cutaways
- Smooth transitions
- Begin color grade (basic correction)

**Day 10-11: Voiceover Recording**
- Professional VO session (studio)
- Record all narration from script
- Multiple takes for options
- Edit VO for pacing and tone
- Integrate into timeline

**Day 12-13: Sound Design**
- Add sound effects (UI beeps, alerts, camera shutters)
- Ambient sound design (wind, traffic, office)
- Music selection and integration
- Audio mixing (dialogue, SFX, music levels)

**Day 14: Color Grading**
- Apply LUTs or color profiles
- Match shots within scenes
- Enhance product colors (green UI, red alerts)
- Cinematic look while maintaining professionalism

### Week 3: Final Delivery

**Day 15-17: Visual Effects & Graphics**
- Motion graphics (logo animations, pricing tables)
- Lower thirds (names, locations)
- Screen overlays (measurement callouts)
- Transitions and effects
- Final polish

**Day 18-19: Final Mix**
- Balance all audio elements
- Mastering (compression, limiting)
- Export stereo mix
- Quality check on different speakers/headphones

**Day 20: Client Approval**
- Deliver final cut for approval
- Make final revisions (minor tweaks only)
- Get sign-off

**Day 21: Render & Deliver**
- Export master files (4K ProRes)
- Create distribution versions:
  - 4K H.264 (YouTube, high quality)
  - 1080p H.264 (website, email)
  - Social media cuts (1:1, 9:16)
  - 60s, 30s, 15s trailers
- Upload to client's delivery platform
- Archive project files

## 🚨 RISK MANAGEMENT

### Common Production Risks:

**Weather Issues:**
- **Risk:** Rain/wind cancels outdoor shots
- **Mitigation:** Schedule buffer days, have indoor backup scenes

**Equipment Failure:**
- **Risk:** Camera/drone malfunction
- **Mitigation:** Backup cameras, test all equipment day before

**Talent No-Show:**
- **Risk:** Actor/driver unavailable
- **Mitigation:** Confirm 24 hours before, have backup talent on call

**Permit Denial:**
- **Risk:** Location unavailable on shoot day
- **Mitigation:** Scout backup locations, apply for permits early

**Insufficient Coverage:**
- **Risk:** Miss critical shots, can't complete edit
- **Mitigation:** Shot cards, dedicated script supervisor, review dailies

**Budget Overrun:**
- **Risk:** Unexpected costs exceed budget
- **Mitigation:** 10% contingency, track expenses daily

### Post-Production Risks:

**Client Revision Cycles:**
- **Risk:** Endless revisions extend timeline
- **Mitigation:** Limit to 2 revision rounds in contract

**Music Licensing Issues:**
- **Risk:** Copyright claims on YouTube
- **Mitigation:** Use only licensed/royalty-free music, keep receipts

**File Corruption:**
- **Risk:** Lost footage or project files
- **Mitigation:** Triple backup (camera card, SSD, cloud)
